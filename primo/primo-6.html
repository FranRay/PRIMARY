PRIMO

We ate, we talked, we laughed, and we had some damn good hot cocoa. I can’t remember the last time I felt this light.

I tell them I want to drive to the hilltop just outside of town to see the sunset. I think we’d only been there a handful of times. There is a comfortable silence that falls on us and it feels like a warm hug telling me it was all finally okay.

Maddox breaks it by asking to play music, “Rylie, put some Phoebe on. You know the one”. Our song. The one Maddox said sounded like being at the edge of an apocalypse. She sure knows how to fit the mood.

‘I Know the End’ by Phoebe Bridgers plays softly in the speakers. Rylie asks, “Hey, what time is it? Isn’t it a few minutes to the end of the world?”. I snicker while my Holophone lights up - “It’s 6:23 pm”, I answer.

“Ooooo, 2 minutes”, Maddox teases.

And you know what, I can’t really tell you because it was all wrapped up in a literal flash.
