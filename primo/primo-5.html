PRIMO

I decided to go to Frank’s anyway - the diner we used to go to every single day after school. I’m all by myself but I mean, I might as well.

I notice how dull the wind sounds. It doesn’t feel like wind that carries life from one place to another - it sounds lulled, faint, almost like an empty whisper. By the corner of my eye, I catch a piece of yellow fabric by a booth seat. It looks so familiar.

It’s… Rylie’s jacket. Does that mean he was recently here? You idiot! .
