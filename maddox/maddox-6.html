MADDOX

“Rylie, put some Phoebe on. You know the one.” We’re in the car now. Primo said something about watching the sunset by the hilltop.

‘I Know the End’ by Phoebe Bridgers plays softly in the speakers. Rylie asks,
“Hey, what time is it? Isn’t it a few minutes to the end of the world?”
“It’s 6:23 pm,” Primo answers.

“Ooooo, 2 minutes.” I tease.

There was a moment of silence there that I definitely caught. Well, not actual silence because there was a song playing. It was silence that made me feel a little too heavy for my liking. Maybe it was a shared and unspoken fear of the world actually ending while we were riding Rylie’s busted ass car to the hills.

I realised in those few moments that I never really thought about what that meant. Up until that point, I just brushed it off as a lame hoax that everyone’s just panicking over. I remember talking to Rylie about that briefly when we bumped into each other. He was scared to death. Understandably so, considering his tendency to literally look over his shoulder everywhere he goes.
