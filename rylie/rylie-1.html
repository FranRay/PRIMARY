RYLIE

If I could describe the essence of my being in one word, it would be ‘constant’. For better or for worse, that is what I have always been – to people in my life, and with how I choose to live my own. Sometimes I wonder if that’s a good thing or not, especially since it makes me fear any change that tries to make its way into my normal.

My grandma’s flower shop has been my biggest constant. She took me in when I was barely old enough to mutter a single sensible word. I grew up in that store and sometimes I think I know more flower species than other human names.

Another constant is my friends. I only have two of them but I think that’s enough to keep me going. Have…? Had…? I- whatever, that doesn’t matter. Or at least, it hasn’t had to matter for a long time.

I met both of them in the same flower shop. Primo, the ambitious one, talked to me first. He noticed the old Captain America toy I was holding and he told me I was cool. I think if he were a colour, he would be red.

And then I met Maddox when she burst into the store panicking and telling us to hide her somewhere. Apparently, she was running away from something really bad she did to her parents. That’s weird, I don’t think she ever told me what it was about .
